# ImproveIt.Today — Teaser Trailer

**Working title:** *"The World, One Fix at a Time"*
**Format:** Teaser trailer — ~90 seconds
**Logline:** From orbit, the Earth looks whole. Up close, it's a billion small problems — and a billion people who could fix them, if only they had a way to be heard.

---

## COLD OPEN — THE BLUE MARBLE (0:00–0:12)

**VISUAL:** Black. Then a single point of blue light. We pull back — it's Earth, from deep space. Silent. Whole. Beautiful. No borders, no labels. Just the planet, turning slowly, the terminator line sweeping day into night.

**SOUND:** Near silence. A low, resonant hum — the sound of something vast. A single piano note.

**TEXT (small, lower third):** *From up here, everything looks fine.*

---

## THE DESCENT (0:12–0:30)

**VISUAL:** The camera begins to fall toward Earth — fast, cinematic, cloud layers whipping past. As we plunge through the atmosphere, tiny points of amber light begin to appear across the darkened continents. Dozens. Then hundreds. Then thousands. Each one a flicker.

**VO (warm, grounded — think a documentary narrator, not a salesman):**
> "Seven billion people. Every one of them walks past something, every single day, that could be better."

**SOUND:** The hum swells. A heartbeat rhythm enters underneath.

---

## GROUND LEVEL — THE ONE (0:30–0:48)

**VISUAL:** We land. Hard cut to street level, a real neighborhood at dusk. A woman — MARIA, 40s, tired, holding her kid's hand — stops at a corner. A streetlight is dark. The crosswalk is black. She looks up at it. She's looked up at it a hundred times.

Then — she takes out her phone. Snaps a photo. A soft chime.

**VISUAL:** Above her head, in the same amber as the orbital shot, a single point of light blinks on.

**TEXT:** *One report.*

**SOUND:** The heartbeat softens. Hopeful.

---

## THE RIPPLE (0:48–1:05)

**VISUAL:** Rapid, rhythmic montage — each beat a real person, a real place, a real problem:
- A teenager in Nairobi upvoting a flooded road on his phone. *(a light brightens)*
- A retired electrician in Lisbon reading a job notification, nodding. *(a light pulses)*
- Neighbors on a stoop pooling small amounts on a screen — €5, €10, €20 climbing. *(the light grows)*
- A city worker's dashboard lighting up with a threshold alert. *(the light turns green)*

**VO:**
> "What if being ignored... was no longer an option? What if the pothole, the dark corner, the broken thing everyone sees and no one fixes — had somewhere to go?"

**SOUND:** Music builds — strings joining the piano, momentum gathering.

---

## THE FIX (1:05–1:18)

**VISUAL:** Back to Maria's corner. Daytime now. A crew finishes wiring the streetlight. It flickers — then blazes to life. Maria and her kid walk under it that evening, lit, safe. She smiles at her phone: a green checkmark.

**VISUAL:** Her point of light on the map flares bright green — and then, around it, others begin turning green too. A chain reaction.

**VO:**
> "One problem solved doesn't stay one problem."

---

## THE PULL-BACK — REPRISE (1:18–1:32)

**VISUAL:** We rise again — the reverse of the opening. Off the street, above the city, into the clouds, back into orbit. But now, as Earth fills the frame, the amber flickers are turning green, one after another, spreading across continents like sunrise. The planet, healing in points of light.

**SOUND:** Full score. The hum from the opening returns — but resolved, major key.

**VO (quiet, certain):**
> "Change the world? No. Change your corner of it. Seven billion corners... is the world."

---

## LOGO / CTA (1:32–1:40)

**VISUAL:** The green lights resolve into the shape of the logo. Clean. Black background.

**TEXT (appearing in sequence):**
> **Report. Vote. Fund. Solve.**
> **ImproveIt.Today**
> *Your world. Starting now.*

**SOUND:** One final piano note. Cut to black.

---

## PRODUCTION NOTES

- **The amber → green motif is the whole film.** Amber = a problem seen. Green = a problem solved. Never break this color grammar; it lets a global-scale idea read in a single frame.
- **The "whole → one → whole" structure** mirrors the product's actual thesis: local action, global impact. The trailer *is* the value proposition, told as emotion instead of features.
- **Casting for truth, not gloss.** Maria should feel like a real person the viewer knows, not a model. The power is in ordinariness.
- **Restraint on UI.** Show phones and screens only in glimpses — the story is human, the app is the enabler. Never let the trailer become a product demo.
- **Music is a character.** The opening hum and closing hum are the same sound, one unresolved and one resolved — the emotional bookend.
